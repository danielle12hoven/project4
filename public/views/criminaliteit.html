FAQ New York


    Do I need a passport to travel to the United States?

    Yes. You need to be in posession of a legal/valid passport (expiration date that remains valid for your entire stay in the US).



    Do I need a VISA to move to the United States?

    Stays < 90 days

    Yes, for any stay in the US that is less than 90 days you will need to acquire an ESTA travel pass (this is the travel VISA).

    You can find more information and register yourself here via ESTA: AllesAmerika.


    Stays > 90 days

    For stays longer than 90 days (internships, study abroad, and full-time jobs) you will need to acquire a VISA.

    Internships:

    - B-1 VISA (for research internship at universities))

    - H-2B VISA (the 'Temporary Worker' visa)

    - H-3 VISA (the 'trainee' visa)

    - J-1 VISA (the 'exchange visitor' visa - valid for 1.5 years)

    - Q VISA (the 'international cultural exchange visitor" visa


    Study Abroad:

    - F-1 VISA

    - J-1 VISA (the 'exchange visitor' visa - valid for 1.5 years)


    Full-Time Job holders:

    - H-1B VISA (valid for 3 years)

    - E-2 VISA (the investors VISA, for individuals with a decent starting capital)



    Will my health insurance cover me while abroad?

    There are 2 options:

    - Keep your Dutch insurance and check to see if it will cover you abroad.

    If you are traveling for leisure, then this is usually the best option.

    - Get American insurance that will cover all of your needs while abroad.



    Can I rent a car with my Dutch licence?

    Yes, just make sure to get car insurance each time you rent.
